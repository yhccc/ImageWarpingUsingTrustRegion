A coarse-to-fine image warping approach using trust region optimization for orthoimage mosaicking

Abstract

Orthoimage mosaicking usually tries to make the seamline bypass the obvious objects as much as possible to produce a seamless digital orthophoto map (DOM). Nevertheless, stitching artifacts may still occur when obvious objects are inevitably crossed by the seamline or when the orthoimages are not accurately aligned in geometry. Thus, image warping is necessary to correct the geometric misalignments in the stitching result. Existing image warping approaches primarily focus on natural images, with relatively few studies targeting orthoimages. Unlike natural images, orthoimages have significant geometric attributes and no longer satisfy the epipolar constraint. Therefore, most existing image warping methods are ineffective in dealing with geometric misalignment in orthoimage mosaicking. To solve the above problems, we propose a coarse-to-fine image warping approach using trust region optimization for orthoimage mosaicking, which is based on photometric and gradient consistency. This approach removes apparent geometric misalignments while preserving most geometric information in orthoimages. Firstly, we obtain an optimal seamline and search for geometrically misaligned regions along the seamline using structural similarity (SSIM). Secondly, we extend a buffer zone centered on the seamline for each region. The geometric alignment problem is modeled as an optimization problem of the deformation vectors for all pixels within the buffer. Next, the trust region method is employed to solve this nonlinear optimization problem from coarse to fine iteratively. By introducing an image pyramid, the optimization from coarse to fine improves efficiency and avoids getting trapped in local optima. Finally, the input orthoimages are warped based on the optimized pixel deformation vectors. However, when dealing with misaligned regions covered by multiple images, the frame-to-frame warping cannot achieve the optimal result. Therefore, we propose a multi-image joint optimization strategy, which introduces auxiliary variables that fuse information from multiple orthoimages to generate seamless multi-image mosaicking results. The experimental results demonstrate that whether in two orthoimage mosaicking or multiple orthoimage mosaicking, our solution has better results in visual quality and quantitative evaluation.
